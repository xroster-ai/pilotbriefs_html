Frequently asked questions

Is this safe to use in the flight deck?

PilotBrief is a study & reference aid. It reproduces your manuals verbatim and cites the exact page, but it is not a replacement for the official approved documentation or your operator's procedures. Always verify against your current approved manuals and apply CRM.

Where do my manuals go?

Nowhere. PilotBrief runs on your own Mac or PC and your documents never leave your computer. You bring your own AI key, and questions go directly from your machine to the provider — never through us.

Does it really work offline?

Yes. Index your manuals once, add a small free on-device model, and PilotBrief answers from your library with no internet and no API key — including scenario reasoning, briefs, and study tools.

What do I need to run it?

A Mac or Windows computer and your own OpenAI and/or Anthropic API key (a few dollars of usage covers heavy use). A free local model is supported for offline use.

One price — what's included?

Everything: grounded cited answers, scenario decision-support, hands-free voice, offline AI, OCR & multi-format import, airport briefs, and the study tools. One licence activates one device for life with no renewal.

Yes. This is the official PilotBrief, built and sold by XRoster — the crew-management platform for airline pilots and cabin crew. The only official site is pilotbriefs.com, and the app is purchased here through secure Stripe checkout. If you see PilotBrief offered anywhere else, it isn't us.

Which aircraft and airlines does it work with?

Any of them. PilotBrief is built around your documents, so it works with any aircraft type (Boeing 737/777/787, Airbus A320/A330/A350 and more) and any airline's OM/SOP — you simply load your own FCOM, QRH, FCTM and company manuals. It understands both Boeing-style QRH indexes and Airbus ECAM structure.
